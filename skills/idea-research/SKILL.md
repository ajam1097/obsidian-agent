# Idea Research

Use this skill when an `idea` page needs a focused research pass before someone decides whether to shape, park, reject, or promote it.

## Inputs

- The idea page.
- The specific question to answer.
- Any source pages or web references the user wants considered.

## Steps

1. Read the idea page and identify its current `idea_type`, `stage`, `status`, and `research_status`.
2. Restate the research question in one sentence.
3. Gather only the context needed to answer that question.
4. Produce a dated research synthesis with:
   - useful findings,
   - tradeoffs,
   - open questions,
   - recommended next status or stage.
5. Propose frontmatter changes separately from body content.

## Rules

- Do not promote an idea automatically.
- Do not set `research_status: requested` yourself; that is the user's authorization signal.
- Mark uncertainty and distinguish source claims from assistant judgment.
- If the research implies a project, propose the project outcome and wait for approval.
- Keep the synthesis short enough that the idea page remains readable.
